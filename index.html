Perhaps you've heard of a whole foods plant based diet but didn't quite understand what it implied. Vegan? Not exactly.

While a vegan diet eliminates dairy, meats, poultry, eggs and fish, there's plenty of room for junk food, or food that has been 'particled'

Junk Food ?

Junk food can be roughly defined to include any edible that provides empty calories, or calories that do not have a nutritional value.

For instance, a candy bar, while tasting delicious, will provide fat and sugar calories, but little nutritional value.

Let's take a pretzel, for instance. It could be considered as a particled food. Its components are flour, salt and water, for the most part. Doesn't sound too harmful. But what are we eating? If it isn't wholewheat flour, we are missing fiber, and any other nutrient that comes with wholewheat, a whole food.

French fries, just potatoes fried in oil, are vegan, but are they a whole food? Unless they are cooked in their skin, they are missing valuable vitamins. Frying in oil loads the potato with fat calories - not a desirable trait! So, while vegan, the fries are not an optimal wholefood.
